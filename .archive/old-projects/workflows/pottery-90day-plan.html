Critical Success Factors

1. Photography (Non-Negotiable)

Minimum Standard:
  • Clean white or light wood background
  • Natural light (no flash, no harsh shadows)
  • Multiple angles (front, top, close-up detail, scale comparison)
  • Lifestyle shots (in use: mug with coffee, bowl with fruit, planter with plant)

Upgrade if budget allows:

  • Lightbox (£30-50)
  • Phone tripod (£15-25)
  • Editing app subscription (£5-10/month)

---

2. Production Consistency

Batch Production Schedule (Example):
  • Monday-Tuesday: Throwing (40 pieces)
  • Wednesday: Trimming + handles
  • Thursday: Bisque firing loading (from last week)
  • Friday: Glazing
  • Saturday-Sunday: Glaze firing (or use studio firing service)

Key: Treat like a job, not a hobby. 25-30 hours/week minimum.

---

3. Pricing Confidence

Do NOT underprice. £50-100 is appropriate for:
  • Handmade in London (high cost of living)
  • Quality materials (stoneware, food-safe glazes)
  • Functional art (not mass-produced)
  • Your time and skill

Comparison:

  • Not On The High Street potter mugs: £28-55 (wholesale 50% = maker gets £14-27)
  • Etsy handmade mugs (UK): £35-80
  • Gallery ceramics: £60-150+

Your pricing is fair. Don't compete with Ikea.

Red Flags to Watch For

Signs You're Off Track:

1. Inconsistent production: Can't keep up with orders = raise prices or reduce channels 2. Burnout: Working 60+ hours/week = unsustainable, reassess 3. Low Instagram engagement: Followers growing but no sales = content isn't converting 4. Etsy views dropping: Algorithm change or competition = improve SEO/photos 5. Stockist non-payment: Net 60 days turns into 90+ = demand upfront payment or stop supplying

Course Corrections:

  • If Etsy fails: Focus Instagram + markets + wholesale
  • If markets unprofitable: Focus Etsy + Instagram + wholesale
  • If Instagram doesn't convert: Focus Etsy + markets + Not On The High Street
  • If wholesale slow: Focus direct-to-consumer (higher margins, immediate payment)

No single channel needs to work. Mix and match to your strengths.

Final Thoughts

This is achievable. 40-50 pieces/month at £50-100 is realistic for a committed potter.

It will take 4-6 months (not 3) to hit consistent £3K/month - allow for learning curve, seasonal fluctuations, and building reputation.

Focus on fundamentals: 1. Great photos (sell the piece before they touch it) 2. Consistent production (treat like a job, not a hobby) 3. Multi-channel (don't rely on one platform) 4. Customer relationships (Instagram Stories, market interactions, emails) 5. Fair pricing (you're not Primark, you're an artist-maker)

You don't need to be on every platform. Find 2-3 channels that work for you (personality, skills, time) and dominate them.

Success pattern: Etsy for steady baseline + Instagram for brand + Markets for cash flow + Wholesale for volume = £3K/month.

Start this week. Day 1 tasks are actionable right now. Don't wait for "perfect" - launch, learn, adjust.

Good luck. You've got this. 🏺
