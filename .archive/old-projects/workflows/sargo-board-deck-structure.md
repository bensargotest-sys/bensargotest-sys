## Talking Points for Tough Questions

### **Q: "What if Variant doesn't lead?"**
**A:** We have a Plan B - pivot to batch VCs (8 in diligence) and extend runway with bridge notes from existing angels. It's messier and takes longer, but it's viable. Charles has committed $100K regardless, and Base grant could provide another $50K-$150K bridge. Worst case, we go to Option 1 (skeleton crew) and extend runway to Q3.

### **Q: "Why should we believe you can close 2 more clients by March?"**
**A:** We have 5 active conversations at various stages. We've already closed 1 client, so we know the motion. 2 out of 5 is a 40% close rate - that's conservative for B2B sales at this stage. If we only close 1, we adjust targets but the story still holds (proof of repeatability).

### **Q: "What if the team quits before funding closes?"**
**A:** Honest answer: it's a risk. We've been transparent with the team - they know the runway situation. They took 50% cuts because they believe in the mission, but we can't abuse that trust past April. If someone leaves, we'll backfill with contractors or adjust scope. But the best retention plan is closing funding fast.

### **Q: "Is this really a $1.5M problem, or just delaying the inevitable?"**
**A:** Fair question. This is not a "buy time" raise - this is a "scale proven traction" raise. We have a retained client, a working product, and a clear path to Series A metrics. $1.5M gives us 9-12 months to hit $1M ARR / 20 clients - that's a fundable Series A. If we can't hit that with $1.5M and 12 months, then yes, we have a business model problem. But we believe we can.

### **Q: "Why not just do the acqui-hire now and save everyone time?"**
**A:** Because we have real traction and a real shot at building a big business. Acqui-hire is the safety net, not the plan. We've delivered on every commitment since Dec (retained client, Facebook Live, ZK PoC). The team wants to see this through. If funding doesn't close by March 31, we'll revisit - but we're not giving up while momentum is on our side.

### **Q: "What's the upside case if everything goes right?"**
**A:** If we hit our 90-day targets (Base launch, 2 more clients, ZK demo, 3x volume), we're on track for Series A in Q4 2026 at $XXM-$XXM valuation. That's a 3-5x markup from this bridge round. If we scale to $1M ARR by EOY, we're looking at a $10M+ Series A. Upside is we build a real business, not just a lifestyle company.

## Deck Delivery Tips

### **Tone Calibration**
- **Confident:** "We have traction and a clear plan"
- **Realistic:** "Runway is tight and risks are real"
- **Respectful:** "Team has sacrificed - we owe them a shot"

### **Body Language**
- Make eye contact - especially on hard slides (Slide 11: Runway Reality, Slide 15: Risks)
- Don't rush through contingency plan (Slide 16) - own it
- Pause after Slide 5 (Team Sacrifice) - let it land

### **Pacing**
- Spend 60% of time on Act 1 & Act 2 (proof + plan)
- Spend 30% on Act 3 (ask + path forward)
- Spend 10% on Q&A setup (acknowledge risks proactively)

### **Pre-Meeting Prep**
- Send deck 24 hours in advance (gives board time to digest)
- Include appendix slides but don't present unless asked
- Have backup data ready (detailed financials, customer references, competitor teardowns)

## Success Metrics for This Deck

**Primary Goal:** Board commits to supporting $1.5M raise by March 31  
**Secondary Goal:** Board provides intro to 2-3 additional investors (backup pipeline)  
**Stretch Goal:** Board member co-invests or intros lead investor (Variant or similar)

**Red Flags (abort signals):**
- Board is skeptical of client pipeline → pivot to "prove 1 more close first"
- Board thinks team will quit → address retention immediately
- Board wants contingency plan NOW → we're in trouble, execute Option 1/2 fast

## Final Check: Does This Deck Pass the "So What?" Test?

- **Slide 1-5:** So what? → We have proof this works (retained client, shipped product, team delivered under pressure)
- **Slide 6-10:** So what? → We have a clear 90-day plan to scale (Base, 2 clients, ZK, 3x volume)
- **Slide 11-16:** So what? → We need $1.5M by March 31 to execute, here's what it buys, here's what happens if we don't get it

**Pass? Yes.** Story is clear, ask is specific, risks are honest.
