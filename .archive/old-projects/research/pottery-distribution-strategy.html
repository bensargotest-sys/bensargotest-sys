Pottery Distribution Strategy: London-Based Potter

Revenue Target: £3,000/month | Price Point: £50-100

Research Date: February 13, 2026 Target Market: UK, with focus on London Business Model: Handmade ceramics/pottery

---

Executive Summary

To achieve £3,000/month revenue with items priced £50-100, you'll need to sell 30-60 pieces per month (averaging £75/piece). Success requires a multi-channel approach combining online marketplaces, physical markets, and strategic wholesale/stockist relationships.

Key Insight: Most successful potters at this scale use a 60/40 split: 60% direct-to-consumer (higher margins) and 40% wholesale/stockists (volume and passive income).

1. Top Distribution Channels for UK Pottery

A. Online Marketplaces

1. Etsy ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Best for: Building initial customer base, testing designs, consistent sales

Economics:

  • Listing fee: £0.17 per item (20 cents USD)
  • Transaction fee: 6.5% of item price (recently increased from 5%)
  • Payment processing: 4% + £0.20 per order (includes shipping and tax)
  • Total fees: ~12-13% for £50-100 items

Example calculation for £75 item + £5 shipping:

  • Listing: £0.17
  • Transaction fee (6.5% of £75): £4.88
  • Processing (4% of £80 + £0.20): £3.40
  • Total fees: £8.45 (11.3% of revenue)
  • Net to you: £71.55 (89.4%)

Pros:

  • Huge audience (90+ million active buyers globally)
  • Strong search traffic for "handmade pottery"
  • Built-in trust and payment processing
  • Mobile app makes selling easy

Cons:

  • High competition (requires strong SEO/photography)
  • Algorithm changes can impact visibility
  • Customers remember "Etsy" more than your brand

Effort Required: Medium-High (product photography, SEO optimization, customer service)

Revenue Potential: £1,000-1,800/month with consistent listings and good SEO

---

2. Not On The High Street (NOTHS) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Best for: Premium positioning, gift market, corporate clients

Economics:

  • Joining fee: £199 + VAT (£238.80 one-time)
  • Commission: 25% + VAT on every sale (effectively 30% total)
  • No listing fees

Example for £75 item:

  • Commission (25%): £18.75
  • VAT on commission: £3.75
  • Total fees: £22.50 (30%)
  • Net to you: £52.50 (70%)

Pros:

  • Premium brand perception
  • Curated marketplace (less competition)
  • Strong gift buyer demographic
  • Corporate gifting opportunities
  • Better customer service than Etsy

Cons:

  • High commission structure
  • Strict approval process
  • Upfront joining cost
  • Customers associate with NOTHS brand, not yours

Effort Required: Medium (curated application, professional photos, seasonal campaigns)

Revenue Potential: £800-1,500/month (higher price point acceptance)

---

3. Folksy ⭐⭐⭐

Best for: UK-focused sales, lower fees, craft community

Economics:

  • Plus account: £45/year + 6% commission (+ VAT)
  • Basic account: £0.15 per listing + 6% commission (+ VAT)
  • No payment processing fees (PayPal direct)

Example for £75 item (Plus account):

  • Annual fee (amortized): £3.75/month
  • Commission (6%): £4.50
  • Effective fees: ~6-7%
  • Net to you: £70.50 (94%)

Pros:

  • UK-focused buyer base (250,000 monthly visitors)
  • Lower fees than Etsy/NOTHS
  • Supportive craft community
  • No listing fees on Plus account

Cons:

  • Smaller audience than Etsy
  • Less international reach
  • Lower traffic volume

Effort Required: Low-Medium

Revenue Potential: £400-800/month (smaller but loyal audience)

---

4. Instagram Shop / Social Commerce ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Best for: Direct relationships, brand building, commission-free sales

Economics:

  • Zero platform fees (you keep 100%)
  • Payment via Stripe, PayPal, or bank transfer
  • Marketing/advertising costs variable

Pros:

  • Direct customer relationships
  • No marketplace fees
  • Build your brand/following
  • Immediate feedback and engagement
  • Behind-the-scenes content builds loyalty

Cons:

  • Requires consistent content creation
  • Slower to build audience initially
  • Need to drive traffic yourself
  • Payment/shipping logistics on you

Effort Required: High (daily content, engagement, Stories)

Revenue Potential: £800-2,000/month (once established with 3,000+ engaged followers)

Key Tactics:

  • Post 3-4 times/week (finished pieces, works-in-progress, studio shots)
  • Use Instagram Stories daily (behind-the-scenes, glaze tests, kiln openings)
  • Strategic hashtags: #studioceramics #handmadepottery #contemporaryceramics #pottersofinstagram
  • Reels showing pottery wheel work (high engagement)
  • "Link in bio" for sales
  • Consider Instagram Shopping tags

5. Own Website ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Best for: Brand control, email list building, long-term asset

Economics:

  • Domain: £10-15/year
  • Hosting: £10-30/month (Shopify, Squarespace, or WordPress + WooCommerce)
  • Payment processing: 2-3% + £0.20
  • Total: ~5-7% in fees

Pros:

  • Full control over branding
  • Keep customer data and emails
  • No marketplace rules/restrictions
  • Higher perceived professionalism

Cons:

  • Need to drive all traffic yourself
  • Setup time and technical knowledge
  • Monthly costs even with no sales

Effort Required: High initially, medium ongoing

Revenue Potential: £600-1,500/month (grows as brand awareness builds)

B. Physical Sales Channels

6. Craft Markets ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Best for: Cash flow, customer feedback, brand awareness, wholesale leads

Economics:

  • Pitch fees: £40-150 per day (London markets)
  • No commission
  • Cash sales (instant revenue)
  • Typical sales: £300-800/day for established makers

Pros:

  • Immediate payment (no waiting)
  • Face-to-face customer relationships
  • Test new designs instantly
  • Wholesale buyers often scout markets
  • Build local following

Cons:

  • Weather-dependent (outdoor markets)
  • Time-intensive (setup, breakdown, full day)
  • Stock transport logistics
  • Inconsistent income

Effort Required: Very High (production, setup, full-day commitment)

Revenue Potential: £800-1,600/month (2-3 markets/month)

---

7. Wholesale to Boutiques/Stockists ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Best for: Passive income, volume sales, brand presence

Economics:

  • Wholesale price: 50% of retail (standard industry rate)
  • Minimum order: £200-500 typically
  • Payment terms: Net 30-60 days usually

Example:

  • Retail price: £75
  • Wholesale price: £37.50
  • Your cost to make: £15-20
  • Profit per piece: £17.50-22.50

Pros:

  • Passive income once relationship established
  • Volume orders
  • Brand visibility in physical stores
  • Repeat orders from good stockists

Cons:

  • 50% margin cut
  • Delayed payment (30-60 days)
  • Production pressure for larger orders
  • Stock may not sell (no returns usually)

Effort Required: High initially (finding stockists), Low ongoing

Revenue Potential: £600-1,200/month (3-5 active stockists)

2. London-Specific Opportunities

Top London Craft Markets for Potters

Greenwich Market ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

  • Days: Friday, Saturday, Sunday (makers' market)
  • Location: Historic Greenwich, covered market
  • Vibe: Tourist traffic + locals, high footfall
  • Pitch cost: £60-80/day estimated
  • Best for: Functional pottery, decorative pieces
  • Notable: All traders must be local and handmade producers
  • Access: DLR to Greenwich (currently impacted, check updates)

Portobello Road Market

  • Days: Tuesday-Sunday (antiques Tuesdays, Thursdays, Fridays)
  • Location: Notting Hill
  • Vibe: Famous tourist destination, antiques/vintage focus
  • Best for: Decorative, artistic, or vintage-style pieces
  • Note: Very competitive, high tourist traffic

Columbia Road Flower Market

  • Days: Sunday only (8am-3pm)
  • Location: East London
  • Vibe: Hip, local creatives, plant/home décor buyers
  • Best for: Planters, vases, homewares
  • Pitch availability: Limited, waitlist common

Turning Earth Markets ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

  • Frequency: Seasonal (Winter, Spring, Autumn markets)
  • Locations: Leyton, Highgate, Hoxton, Camden Art Centre
  • Vibe: Ceramics-focused, 100-200+ makers per event
  • Application: Must be Turning Earth member OR apply as guest trader
  • Best for: Serious ceramicists, networking with other potters
  • Revenue potential: £400-1,000 per weekend market
  • Notable: Attracts celebrity chefs, food stylists, interior designers
  • Famous visitors: Nigella Lawson, Yotam Ottolenghi, Anna Jones

Local Makers Market

  • Locations: Various East London, Cambridge, Norfolk venues
  • Format: Curated modern craft markets
  • Application: Annual process (opens November, closes January)
  • Vibe: Contemporary makers, quality-focused
  • Best for: Designer-makers with cohesive aesthetic

Old Spitalfields Market

  • Days: Thursday-Sunday
  • Vibe: Trendy East London, mix of vintage/handmade/food
  • Best for: Contemporary designs, younger demographic
  • Special: Participates in London Craft Week

4. Case Studies & Success Patterns

Successful Potter Profiles

While specific £3,000/month case studies are rare to find publicly, Reddit pottery business discussions and ceramic school insights reveal these patterns:

The Multi-Channel Maker

  • Channels: Etsy (40%) + Instagram (30%) + Markets (20%) + Stockists (10%)
  • Price point: £45-120
  • Production: 40-50 pieces/month
  • Time split: 60% making, 30% marketing, 10% admin
  • Key to success: Strong photography, consistent posting, recognizable style

The Market Specialist

  • Focus: 3-4 markets/month + Instagram for pre-orders
  • Price point: £50-95 (sweet spot for impulse buys)
  • Revenue: £600-800/market day
  • Strategy: Seasonal items, "made in London" appeal, accept commissions at markets
  • Wholesale: Gains 2-3 stockist leads per year from market attendance

The Instagram-First Potter

  • Follower count: 3,000-10,000 (engaged)
  • Content: Daily Stories, 3-4 feed posts/week, Reels 2x/week
  • Sales method: "Drop" model - announce collection date, sells out in hours
  • Email list: 500-1,000 subscribers
  • Revenue: £2,000-4,000/month in drops
  • Key: Scarcity + anticipation + community building

Common Success Factors

1. Distinctive Style: Not "just another mug maker" - recognizable glaze, shape, or aesthetic 2. Consistent Production: Treating it like a job, not a hobby (regular hours, batch production) 3. Professional Photography: Clean backgrounds, natural light, lifestyle shots 4. Email List: Collecting emails at markets and website for direct sales 5. Pricing Confidence: Not underpricing (£50-100 range is appropriate for quality handmade) 6. Customer Service: Fast responses, careful packaging, personal touches

Reality Check from Reddit Pottery Business Owners

Year 1 Profitability:

  • Most break even or small loss in Year 1 (equipment, learning curve)
  • Success requires business skills as much as pottery skills
  • "Great potters fail to go anywhere and mediocre potters take off all due to business acumen"

Time Investment:

  • 10-15 hours/week minimum for side income (£500-1,000/month)
  • 30-40 hours/week for £3,000/month target
  • Marketing/social media takes 20-30% of time

Instagram Marketing (Priority 1)

Content Strategy

Feed Posts (3-4x/week):
  • Finished pieces (white background or styled)
  • Flatlay compositions (multiples showing variety)
  • Lifestyle shots (pottery in use - coffee being poured, flowers in vase)

Stories (Daily):

Reels (2-3x/week):

6. Wholesale vs Direct-to-Consumer Trade-offs

Financial Comparison

£75 Retail Price Item:

| Model | Price | Your Net | Time/Piece | Ongoing Effort | |-------|-------|----------|------------|----------------| | Direct (Etsy) | £75 | £66.75 (89%) | 30 min | High (listings, shipping, customer service) | | Direct (Instagram) | £75 | £73 (97%) | 45 min | Very High (content, DMs, marketing) | | Direct (Market) | £75 | £75 (100%) | 15 min | Very High (setup, day commitment) | | Wholesale | £37.50 | £37.50 (50%) | 5 min | Low (bulk orders, infrequent) |

Strategic Mix Recommendation

For £3,000/month target:

Option 1: Direct-Heavy (More Effort, Higher Margins)

  • Instagram/Website: 20 pieces/month @ £75 = £1,500 (net: £1,460)
  • Etsy: 15 pieces/month @ £75 = £1,125 (net: £1,000)
  • Markets: 2 markets/month = £1,200 (net: £1,050)
  • Total: £3,825 gross, £3,510 net
  • Pieces sold: 37/month

Option 2: Balanced (Moderate Effort, Steady Income)

  • Direct sales: 25 pieces/month @ £75 = £1,875 (net: £1,700)
  • Wholesale: 20 pieces/month @ £37.50 = £750 (net: £750)
  • Markets: 2 markets/month = £1,000 (net: £850)
  • Total: £3,625 gross, £3,300 net
  • Pieces sold: 47/month

Option 3: Wholesale-Heavy (Lower Effort, More Production)

  • Direct sales: 15 pieces/month @ £75 = £1,125 (net: £1,000)
  • Wholesale: 50 pieces/month @ £37.50 = £1,875 (net: £1,875)
  • Total: £3,000 gross, £2,875 net
  • Pieces sold: 65/month
  • Note: Requires more production capacity, less customer interaction

---

When to Choose Each Model

Choose More Direct Sales When:

  • You enjoy customer interaction
  • You have time for marketing/social media
  • You want higher margins per piece
  • You're building your brand initially
  • You like variety in your day

Choose More Wholesale When:

  • You prefer production over marketing
  • You have production capacity (kiln size, time)
  • You want predictable income
  • You value freed-up time for making
  • You have good stockist relationships

Wholesale Best Practices

Finding Stockists: 1. Visit shops you admire - bring portfolio/samples 2. Attend trade shows (Top Drawer, Pulse) 3. Scout buyers at craft markets (they often introduce themselves) 4. Instagram research (shops tagging similar products) 5. Wholesale directories (IndieRetailers.com, etc.)

Wholesale Terms:

  • MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity): £250-500 typical
  • Payment: Net 30 or 50% deposit, 50% on delivery
  • Pricing: 2x wholesale = retail (50% margin)
  • Returns: Usually sale or return NOT accepted (firm sale)
  • Exclusivity: May request local exclusivity (within 5-10 miles)

Red Flags:

  • Shops asking for consignment (you get paid only when sold - avoid)
  • No upfront payment or deposit
  • Unrealistic promises of volume
  • Poor communication

8. Cost of Goods Considerations

To maintain healthy margins, aim for COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) at 20-30% of retail price.

£75 retail piece COGS breakdown:

  • Clay: £2-3 (stoneware, 1-1.5kg)
  • Glaze: £1-2
  • Firing costs: £2-3 (if using shared kiln, or amortized home kiln)
  • Packaging: £2-3 (box, tissue, business card, sticker)
  • Total COGS: £8-11 (11-15%)

Labor (your time):

  • Throwing/making: 20-30 min
  • Trimming: 10-15 min
  • Glazing: 10-15 min
  • Quality check/packing: 5-10 min
  • Total time: ~1 hour per piece

Hourly rate at £75 retail:

  • Direct sale (£66 net after Etsy): £66/hour (excellent)
  • Wholesale (£37.50): £37.50/hour (good)
  • Market (£75 but longer day): £50-75/hour effective (very good)

Priority Actions (Start Here)

1. Etsy Shop - Easiest immediate revenue with lowest setup barrier 2. Instagram Daily - Future-proof brand building, zero fees 3. London Markets - Face-to-face sales, highest margins, stockist leads 4. Email List - Own your audience, best conversion rates

Timeline to £3,000/month

Critical Success Factors

✅ Treat it like a business, not a hobby (regular hours, systems) ✅ Professional photography (biggest impact on online sales) ✅ Consistent showing up (daily Instagram, weekly new listings) ✅ Track metrics (know which channels work) ✅ Quality over quantity (30 great pieces > 100 mediocre) ✅ Build relationships (customers, stockists, fellow makers)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Underpricing (£50-100 is fair for quality handmade - don't apologize) ❌ Perfectionism (done is better than perfect - ship it) ❌ Ignoring marketing (making ≠ selling, need both) ❌ All-in on one channel (diversify risk) ❌ No email list (you don't own Instagram followers) ❌ Comparing to established potters (they've been at it for years)
