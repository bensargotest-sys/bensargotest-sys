# Pottery Business Quick-Start Checklist
## Goal: £3,000/month in 4-5 months | 40-50 pieces/month @ £50-100

---

## Week 1: Foundation (Do This First)

### Day 1-2: Photography Setup ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT
- [ ] Create photo corner (white backdrop or light wood surface, natural light)
- [ ] Take 20 test shots of existing pieces
- [ ] Edit 5 best photos (phone apps: Snapseed or VSCO - free)
- [ ] Take 3 "lifestyle" shots (mug with coffee, bowl with food, vase with flowers)

**Why:** Photos are #1 factor in online sales. Great photos = 5x more sales.

---

### Day 3-4: Launch Etsy Shop
- [ ] Create Etsy account (etsy.com/sell)
- [ ] List 10 pieces (5 photos each, SEO titles like "Handmade Ceramic Mug | London Potter | Stoneware Coffee Cup")
- [ ] Set up shipping profiles (UK, Europe, US)
- [ ] Write shop policies (returns, shipping times)

**Cost:** £1.70 (10 listings @ £0.17 each)  
**Expected:** 3-8 sales in first month = £150-600

---

### Day 5-6: Instagram Business Account
- [ ] Convert to Business account (free)
- [ ] Write bio: "London potter | Handmade ceramics | DM to order" + link
- [ ] Post first 9 photos (3 finished pieces, 3 lifestyle, 2 process, 1 studio)
- [ ] Follow 50 similar potters (#londonpotter #studioceramics)
- [ ] Use 15-20 hashtags per post (mix of popular + niche)

**Daily from now on:** 2-3 Instagram Stories + engage with 10 posts

---

### Day 7: Apply to Markets
- [ ] Research Greenwich Market (greenwichmarket.london) - Fri/Sat/Sun, best for potters
- [ ] Research Turning Earth Markets (turningearth.uk/markets) - seasonal, ceramics-focused
- [ ] Create trader profile (3 photos, bio, price range)
- [ ] Apply for Month 2-3 dates

**Market revenue:** £300-600 first market → £600-800 by third market

---

## Weeks 2-4: Build Inventory & Start Selling

### Production (20-25 hours/week)
- [ ] Batch produce 40-60 pieces (mugs, bowls, planters)
- [ ] Document process (photos/videos for Instagram content)
- [ ] Create "signature piece" (recognizable style/glaze)

---

### Instagram Routine (30-45 min/day)
**Daily:**
- 2-3 Stories (morning studio, work-in-progress, finished piece)
- Engage with 10-20 posts (thoughtful comments)

**3x per week (feed posts):**
- Monday: Finished piece (white background)
- Wednesday: Process video (wheel, glazing)
- Friday: "Available now" announcement

**1x per week:**
- Reel (wheel throwing time-lapse, 15-30 seconds, trending audio)

**Goal:** 800-1,200 followers by Month 2 end

---

### Wholesale (Week 3-4)
- [ ] Create 1-page linesheet PDF (products, wholesale prices, minimum £250 order)
- [ ] Visit 3 local boutiques:
  - Made in Greenwich
  - Labour and Wait (Redchurch St)
  - Vessel Gallery (Portobello Rd)
- [ ] Buy something small, ask who buys for shop, leave linesheet

**Wholesale pricing:** 50% of retail (if retail = £75, wholesale = £37.50)

---

## Month 2-3: Scale to £3K

### Etsy Growth
- Add 5 new listings/month (target 25-30 active)
- Respond to messages within 4 hours (Etsy rewards fast response)
- Request reviews from happy customers
- Test Etsy Ads (£20-30/month if profitable)

**Target:** 20-25 sales/month = £1,000-1,500

---

### Instagram "Drops" (Start Month 2)
**Formula:**
- Monday: Announce "Friday Drop - 10 new pieces, 6pm"
- Throughout week: Teaser photos in Stories
- Friday 6pm: Post to feed + Stories "Link in bio!"
- Sell 8-12 pieces in 2-4 hours

**Do 2 drops/month = £800-1,800/month**

---

### Markets (2-3 per month)
**Bring:**
- 30-40 pieces
- Tablecloth, price tags, business cards
- Card reader (Square or SumUp - essential!)
- Email signup sheet (offer 10% off first order)

**Expected:** £400-700 per market by Month 2-3

---

### Wholesale (2-3 Stockists by Month 3)
- Follow up with shops 2 weeks after first contact
- Deliver first orders on time (4-6 weeks)
- Build relationships = repeat orders

**Expected:** £600-1,200/month passive income

## Critical Success Rules

### 1. Photography is Non-Negotiable
Bad photos = no sales. Great photos = everything sells.

### 2. Batch Production Schedule
Treat like a job, not hobby. 25-30 hours/week minimum:
- Mon-Tue: Throwing (40 pieces)
- Wed: Trimming
- Thu: Bisque firing
- Fri: Glazing
- Sat-Sun: Glaze firing

### 3. Pricing Confidence
£50-100 is FAIR for London handmade ceramics. Don't undersell.
- Your time + skill + materials + London cost of living = worth it
- Not On The High Street mugs: £28-55 (and they take 30% commission!)
- You're an artist-maker, not Ikea

### 4. Multi-Channel Strategy
Don't rely on one platform:
- Etsy = steady baseline
- Instagram = brand building + direct sales
- Markets = cash flow + wholesale leads
- Wholesale = volume + passive income

### 5. Email List from Day 1
- Collect at markets (paper signup)
- Add to Instagram link-in-bio
- Offer 10% off first order
- Email converts 10x better than social media

## Red Flags to Watch For

❌ **Stop if:**
- Working 60+ hours/week (burnout)
- Production can't keep up (raise prices or reduce channels)
- Markets losing money after 3 attempts (focus online)

✅ **You're on track if:**
- Instagram followers growing 100-200/month
- Etsy sales 10+ per month by Month 2
- At least 1 stockist interested by Month 3
- Production rhythm feels sustainable
